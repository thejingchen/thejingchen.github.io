Dum-E: Checkers-Playing Robotic Arm

Spring 2016

ME102B: Mechatronic Design

more info >>

In this senior capstone project, we chose to create a complex robotic arm system that plays checkers with an opponent. It consists of the hardware component (the arm and electronics) and a software component (positioning, computer vision, and gameplay AI).

The arm was modeled in SolidWorks, and the bulk of the arm was laser cut with acrylic. The gripper is controlled with a servo motor, and the arms and base are controlled with a geared stepper motor. The arm is controlled with an Arduino Duemilanove and motor controllers. A Logitech webcam is used for computer vision.

Team Members: Jing Chen, Ricky Lee, Ryan Liu, Jonathan Shum, Bryan Thov, Shun Yao

Zest

Spring 2015

ME110: Introduction to New Product Development

more info >>

Zest is the ultimate kitchen companion: a series of detachable sensors, coupled with a mobile app. It provides active recipe guidance and alerts with hands-free control of the device. This project was sponsored by Samsung.

Zest includes low-fidelity prototypes, app mock-ups, and a video demo. We went through the full product development cycle, including ideation, market research, prototyping and feedback, sustainable design, and business analysis.

Team Members: Candy Chang, Jing Chen, Terence Cho, Ricky Lee, Bryan Thov, Shun Yao

Mechatronic Hand

Spring 2015

ME122: Processing of Materials in Manufacturing

more info >>

Our mechatronic hand was designed with performance-driven situations in mind. The end goal is for the user to be able to throw and catch a tennis ball. This project was focused on researching suitable materials and manufacturing methods for our design.

The hand was modeled in SolidWorks, and the finite element analysis was performed using a SolidWorks program. We used Ashby charts to choose ideal materials, and performed detailed selection analysis for the manufacturing methods. We also gave several technical presentations throughout the course to a panel of graduate students and industry professionals.

Team Members: Jing Chen, Jonathan Shum, Bryan Thov

Projectile Roll Car: CaliBEAR

Fall 2015

ME107: Mechanical Laboratory

more info >>

The task in this mechanical laboratory class was to use the provided roll car track with sensors positioned along the length, and define our own project. We decided to create a projectile system, in which a car on one track would launch a projectile into a target on the other track.

The cars were manufactured in the machine shop, and the launching mechanism was 3D printed with ABS plastic. The car with electronic components utilized a gear motor and infrared sensor, and was controlled with an Arduino Uno. We also used an ultrasonic sensor and an NI Launchpad in a side project.

Team Members: Sofia Arevalo, Jing Chen, Mandy Chen, Jenalee Custodio, Ricky Lee, Jason Norona, Danny Van Ness

Automatic Drink Mixer

Fall 2014

ME130: Design of Planar Machinery

For this planar machinery class, we designed a four-bar mechanism that can perform a certain task. In this case, we created an automatic drink mixer.

The mechanism was modeled in SolidWorks and manufactured in the machine shop. The cup portion was 3D printed with ABS plastic, the linkages were waterjetted in aluminum, and the base was created in the CNC mill out of wood. The mechanism was controlled by a gear motor.

Team Members: Jing Chen, Yunxing Ma, Bowen Xu, Jonathan Shum, Alan Zhang
